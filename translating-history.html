Translating History

Thirty Years on the Front Lines of Diplomacy

In 1987, Ronald Reagan and Mikhail Gorbachev launched into discussionsthat would change the world and the course of global politics. These summit meetings signalled the welcome and inevitable end to the Cold War. They spooke of communism, democracy, and nuclear disarmament. Yet Reagan spoke no Russian, and Gorbachev spoke no English. Igor Korchilov not only facilitated the momentous summit meetings of the lates 1980s but spent nearly thirty years translating for Russian leaders, including five critical years as one of the principal interpreters for Mikhail Gorbachev.
